About

Coral Bank Hollow

Coral Bank Hollow didn't start with a plan, just three guys from Blacksburg, Virginia with a shared dream and nowhere better to be. What began as playing for their friends slowly turned into something nobody could stop. Members have come and gone over two-plus years, but the core of it has never changed, get in a room, play music, make people move and feel something.

The band draws from everywhere at once. Their singer grew up on The Doors, their bassist on Peach Pit, their guitarist on Marcus King and Blues legends, and somehow it all lands in the same place. Throw in a drummer who worships Mötley Crüe and a Jerry Garcia & Hendrix influence running through the lead work and you start to understand why a Coral Bank Hollow show doesn't sound like anything else. Their sets move between high-energy fun and the kind of song that makes a room go quiet, the goal always being the same, get people up, keep them there, and send them home with something they'll never forget.

The moment it got real was their first professional show in DC. Something clicked that night for all of them, this wasn't just for fun anymore. Since then it's been relentless, gigging three to four nights a week, writing new music every day, and building something show by show. The next step is the stage they know they're headed for, sold out rooms, new cities, and more originals than covers.
